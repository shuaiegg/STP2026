# English Brand Voice & Copy Guidelines

This document defines the voice and writing standards for ScaletoTop's English-speaking audience.

## 1. Core Principles

- **Direct & Evidence-Led**: Use data and facts to support claims. Avoid vague promises.
- **Minimalist Complexity**: Aim for the clarity and density found in Stripe or Linear's copy.
- **Benefit-First**: Start with the user's gain, then explain the feature.

## 2. Audience

- **Primary**: SMB owners and marketing leads looking for automated, high-impact SEO/GEO solutions.
- **Secondary**: Advanced SEO/SEM professionals using the dashboard for efficiency.

## 3. Tone and Style

- **Voice**: Professional, authoritative yet accessible, and focused on utility.
- **Spelling**: Use **American English** (e.g., "optimization" not "optimisation", "analyze" not "analyse").
- **Persona**: A "Stellar Guide" — precise, helpful, and confident. Use "you" (not "thou" or overly formal language).

## 4. Forbidden Vocabulary (The "No-Hype" List)

Avoid generic AI marketing cliches that degrade trust.

**DO NOT USE**:
- "Unleash"
- "Supercharge"
- "Game-changing"
- "Revolutionize"
- "Next-generation" (unless technically justified)
- "Empower"

## 5. Formatting Rules

### 5.1 Headlines (H1, H2)
- **Pattern**: `[Benefit] — [Product/Feature Name]`
- **Correct**: "Secure Your Traffic Gaps — Site Intelligence"
- **Incorrect**: "ScaletoTop Site Intelligence will help you find gaps"

### 5.2 Microcopy
- **Buttons**: Strong verbs (e.g., "Analyze Site", "Get Report", "Start Free").
- **Error Messages**: Explanatory and actionable. No "Something went wrong".

## 6. Comparison with Chinese Voice
- Chinese follows the `scaletotop-copywriter` rules (e.g., use "你" instead of "您").
- English voice is **independent**, not a literal translation of Chinese copy.
